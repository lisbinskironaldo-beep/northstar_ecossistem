# Post Launch Infinite Operations

## Purpose

This document governs the post-launch life of the ecosystem starting after the first launch and continuing after all three launches indefinitely.

## Core Principle

Post-launch work is not maintenance only.

Post-launch work is the real product loop.

The ecosystem stays alive if it keeps refreshing value, cleaning decay and evolving structure without losing identity.

## The Attention And Novelty Model

The cadence below is an operational inference based on user-retention benchmarks, habit-formation research and release-strategy evidence.

### Time windows that matter

- Day 0 to Day 3: users decide if there is immediate value
- Day 7: first return rhythm must already exist
- Day 14: weak products start losing identity in the user mind
- Day 30: major retention drop usually defines whether a product has early staying power
- Day 56 to Day 70: habit-strength window where repetition can become much more automatic
- Every 7 days: users need reasons to come back before the habit loop breaks
- Every 4 to 6 weeks: visible freshness is needed to avoid product staleness
- Every quarter: the ecosystem needs a structural step forward, not just cosmetic updates

## Exact Post-Launch Review Moments

For each front after launch, mandatory reviews happen at:

- T+6h
- T+24h
- T+72h
- T+7d
- T+14d
- T+30d
- T+45d
- T+60d
- T+90d
- every 30 days after T+90d

At each moment, the command center must generate a founder briefing with:

- metric shifts
- risk level
- top operational problems
- top content-quality problems
- top creator-supply problems
- recommended actions in priority order

## What This Means In Practice

### After the first launch

The first 30 days are not for expansion.

They are for:

- fixing leaks
- tuning ranking
- improving first-session value
- removing bad content fast
- improving creator onboarding

### From day 30 to day 70

This is the habit-building block.

The product must create:

- weekly content reasons to return
- lightweight visible updates
- small social proof loops
- better recommendations
- clearer progress for creators

### After day 70

If the product still relies only on its launch novelty, it will flatten.

From here, the ecosystem needs scheduled novelty.

## Infinite Cadence Model

### Daily cadence

- catalog cleaning
- trust and safety signals
- ranking recalculation
- support triage
- infra alerts review
- executive pulse delivered at 08:30 America/Sao_Paulo

### Weekly cadence

- new content highlights
- category refreshes
- creator spotlights
- ranking review
- report review
- small UX fixes
- founder operating review every Monday at 09:00 America/Sao_Paulo
- product-quality review every Thursday at 15:00 America/Sao_Paulo

### Biweekly cadence

- recommendation tuning
- feed rule tuning
- creator communication updates
- onboarding optimization

### Every 4 to 6 weeks

- visible novelty release
- new collection or themed lane
- fresh category packaging
- UI polish block
- social or creator challenge

This is the freshness window that prevents the product from feeling static after the launch effect fades.

### Every 42 days

- mandatory novelty planning review
- identify what users will visibly perceive as new
- define whether the novelty is visual, editorial, structural or creator-based

### Every 8 to 10 weeks

- stronger feature drop
- deeper personalization upgrade
- creator progression improvement
- trust system refinement
- category taxonomy review

This matches the rough habit-consolidation window seen in behavioral research and is the right time to give users a larger reason to renew commitment.

### Every 84 days

- structural product improvement
- performance architecture review
- cost model review
- storage policy review
- visual identity refresh audit
- cross-front strategy review

### Every 6 months

- brand perception audit
- creator economy review
- premium strategy review
- talent program review
- launch or no-launch decisions for major new systems

### Yearly cadence

- full ecosystem reset review
- naming and market-position review
- product-line health review
- international expansion review
- talent-to-business conversion review

## Command Center Requirement

All post-launch cadences above must be scheduled and visible inside one founder-facing system.

The founder must never need to remember the next critical review manually.

## Golden Rule Of Post-Launch

Novelty cannot be random.

Novelty must be scheduled, measured and tied to retention goals.

## Sources Used For Cadence Guidance

The cadence above is an inference informed by:

- habit-formation meta-analysis suggesting median habit timelines around 59 to 66 days, with broad variation
- app-retention benchmarks showing the largest drop happens in the first 30 days
- evidence that weekly engagement is strongly associated with long-term retention
- a 2025 Marketing Science field experiment showing weekly episode releases can increase engagement and subscriptions compared with full-season dumps in subscription streaming
