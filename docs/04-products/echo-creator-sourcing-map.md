# Echo Creator Sourcing Map

## Purpose

This document turns creator acquisition into a repeatable sourcing routine for the Echo beta.

## Sourcing Principle

Start where AI music creators are already publishing and discussing their work.

Do not start with mass acquisition.

Start with narrow, high-signal sourcing lanes.

## Primary Lanes

### Lane 1: Short-Form Discovery

Look for:

- AI song previews
- creator posts about new AI tracks
- creators testing multiple identities or sounds

Use this lane for:

- melodic electronic
- trap
- pop-adjacent

### Lane 2: Community Threads

Look for:

- creators sharing finished tracks
- creators asking for feedback
- creators frustrated with distribution limits

Use this lane for:

- lo-fi
- ambient
- experimental
- dark

### Lane 3: Video-Based Proof

Look for:

- creators who already explain or showcase AI music work
- creators with at least some repeat output
- creators who understand their own identity

Use this lane for:

- cinematic
- soundtrack-adjacent
- bilingual or globally legible creators

## Weekly Sourcing Rhythm

### Day 1

- identify `10 to 15` candidate creators
- reject obvious spam or weak-fit candidates immediately

### Day 2

- trim to `6 to 10` high-fit candidates
- prioritize the first invite wave

### Day 3

- send initial outreach
- record source lane and first response

### Day 5

- send one follow-up to good-fit non-responders

### Day 7

- mark accepted, hold, or archive

## Sourcing Rule

A smaller creator with clear identity is more useful than a bigger creator with weak fit.

## Archive Reasons

Archive a candidate if:

- the work feels spam-heavy
- the identity is unclear
- output looks too inconsistent
- the creator seems focused only on instant monetization
- content quality would weaken the opening Echo beta feel
