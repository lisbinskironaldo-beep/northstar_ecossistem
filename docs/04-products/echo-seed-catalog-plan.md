# Echo Seed Catalog Plan

## Purpose

This document defines the first intentional seed catalog for the Echo beta.

The seed catalog exists to prevent an empty or weak first session and to create the minimum quality floor before real creators arrive in volume.

## Beta Catalog Goal

The first listener session must feel like:

- there is already life in the app
- at least a few tracks are worth saving
- the catalog is varied enough to explore
- low-quality repetition is not dominating the experience

## Seed Catalog Target

- total tracks: `80 to 150`
- primary categories covered: `4 to 6`
- creators represented: `8 to 20`
- obvious "save-worthy" tracks: at least `12 to 20`

## Category Mix For Beta

Recommended starting spread:

- atmospheric / ambient
- lo-fi / chill
- melodic electronic
- dark / night
- experimental
- cinematic or narrative-adjacent

Rule:

- do not overload the beta with joke or novelty content
- the beta catalog should prove habit and discovery before proving chaos

## Quality Floor

A seeded track should only stay in the beta catalog if it passes this basic filter:

- title is understandable
- creator identity is coherent
- category makes sense
- audio is not obviously broken
- concept is not generic to the point of feeling disposable
- it would not embarrass the product in a first session

## Remove Before Beta

Remove or archive seeded tracks that are:

- near-duplicates
- filler with no clear value
- badly tagged
- confusing in artist identity
- obviously lower quality than the rest of the catalog

## Catalog Review Process

### Pass 1: Structural Review

Check:

- category spread
- creator spread
- naming quality
- duplicate risk

### Pass 2: Experience Review

Check:

- first 10 tracks of the feed
- whether at least a few tracks feel save-worthy
- whether the feed feels repetitive or thin

### Pass 3: Beta Lock

Decide:

- what stays in the starting catalog
- what moves to reserve
- what should not appear in beta at all

## Minimum Beta Feed Standard

The first screen of the feed should contain:

- at least one track that feels polished
- at least one track with strong identity
- at least one track with broad accessibility
- no cluster of weak or repetitive items

## Reserve Catalog

Keep a reserve set outside the opening feed for:

- replacing weak performers
- refreshing the first session during beta
- testing if variety improves saves

## Success Signal

The catalog is good enough when:

- listeners save naturally
- creators do not immediately perceive the product as empty or low-quality
- the feed feels curated by taste, even before full algorithmic maturity
