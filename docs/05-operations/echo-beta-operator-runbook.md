# Echo Beta Operator Runbook

## Purpose

This runbook defines the minimum operating routine for the controlled Echo beta.

It exists so beta management does not depend on memory or improvisation.

## Operator Goals

During beta, the operator must protect:

- first-session quality
- creator flow clarity
- moderation responsiveness
- trust in the product team's judgment

## Daily Clock

### Morning Review

Check:

- feed health
- saves
- follows
- creator state changes (`invited`, `accepted`, `onboarded`)
- uploads from the last 24 hours
- report queue

### Midday Review

Check:

- unresolved creator friction
- creators stuck between `accepted` and `onboarded`
- weak week-one pulse
- repeated user complaints
- weak or repetitive feed patterns

### End-Of-Day Review

Check:

- what changed today
- what still feels risky
- whether tomorrow needs intervention or observation

## Issue Logging Path

Log every meaningful issue under one of these labels:

- feed quality
- upload friction
- creator confusion
- moderation load
- reporting failure
- trust / spam risk
- catalog weakness

## Escalation Rules

Pause or narrow the beta if:

- uploads are failing repeatedly
- reports are not closing properly
- the feed becomes obviously weak
- creators are confused at the same step repeatedly
- accepted creators are not becoming onboarded
- operator trust in the dashboard drops

## Rollback Rule

If a change creates confusion or instability during beta:

- revert to the last stable product behavior
- document what broke
- do not layer new changes on top of confusion

## Daily Questions

The operator should answer these every day:

- did listeners find something worth saving?
- did creators upload without operator rescue?
- who is still accepted but not truly onboarded?
- did moderation stay manageable?
- did the feed feel more alive or more noisy?
- is the product getting stronger, or only busier?

## Beta End Review

At the end of the beta window, produce:

- product friction summary
- creator summary
- trust summary
- catalog summary
- recommendation: widen, hold, or rerun

## Per-Creator Rule

Once a creator becomes `onboarded`, keep a simple week-one scorecard for that persona.

Do not rely only on memory across days.
