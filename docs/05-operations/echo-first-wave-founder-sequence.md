# Echo First-Wave Founder Sequence

## Purpose

This file is the practical founder sequence for turning the first real creators from:

- `candidate`
- to `invited`
- to `accepted`
- to `onboarded`

It exists so the first wave is not handled by improvisation.

## Core Rule

The first wave is not a growth blast.

It is a controlled learning wave.

That means:

- small
- responsive
- honest
- easy to inspect

## Step 1: Invite

When reaching out:

- explain Echo in one clean sentence
- explain why the creator was chosen
- explain the ask clearly
- keep the ask small

Do not:

- overpromise
- talk about money
- make it sound finished

## Step 2: Acceptance

When a creator says yes:

- mark `accepted`
- send the beta packet
- ask for one first track, not a whole catalog

The goal here is motion, not volume.

## Step 3: First Product Contact

Watch:

- did the creator choose a persona?
- did the creator complete the minimum bio?
- did the creator publish one main-room track?

If not, they are still `accepted`, not `onboarded`.

## Step 4: Onboarding Bar

Move to `onboarded` only when:

- active persona selected
- basic bio present
- at least `1` active main-room track
- at least `2` tracks in the workspace
- creator understands active versus sleeping catalog logic

## Step 5: Week-One Read

Once onboarded, read:

- `Abertura`
- `Folga ativa`
- `Publicadas`
- `Reserva`

And answer:

- are they moving naturally?
- are they clogging the catalog?
- do they need rescue?
- are they likely to upload again?

## Step 6: End Of First Week

Sort each creator into one of these:

- strong fit
- promising but confused
- low return likelihood
- hold for later

## Final Rule

The founder should optimize for:

- learning speed
- creator honesty
- low rescue

not for inflated counts.
