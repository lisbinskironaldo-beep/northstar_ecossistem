# Governing Master Document

## Purpose

This document governs the full Northstar Ecosystem and should be treated as the strategic source of truth for the three public fronts and the central operating system behind them.

## Ecosystem Identity

- Company codename: `Northstar Ecosystem`
- Product 1 codename: `Echo` (`Music IA`)
- Product 2 codename: `Pulse` (`Shorts IA`)
- Product 3 codename: `Lumen` (`Watch IA`)
- Internal command layer codename: `Northstar Command Center`

These names are working names and can change later. The structure must not.

## Core Thesis

Northstar will not be a creation tool.

Northstar will be a discovery, distribution and talent-evolution ecosystem for creators of AI-native content.

The market-facing strategy is three separate products.

The operating strategy is one central platform.

The management strategy is one command layer with AI-assisted decision support.

## Strategic Rules

1. Sustainability before profit.
2. Survival before scale bursts.
3. Global ambition from day zero.
4. Public products separated by intent.
5. Core systems unified by design.
6. Entry can be open; reach can never be unlimited.
7. Community helps quality, but cannot be the only filter.
8. Dead content cannot consume live-product resources forever.
9. New fronts open only after previous readiness gates are met.
10. Talent discovery is a business line, not a side effect.
11. Every important change must have a scheduled review time.
12. Management must be assisted by system intelligence, not only manual monitoring.

## Why The Three-Front Model Exists

### Echo / Music IA

Purpose:

- validate creator supply
- create repeat consumption
- test discovery quality
- build catalog discipline

### Pulse / Shorts IA

Purpose:

- accelerate awareness
- create fame loops
- increase creator discovery speed
- drive external growth

### Lumen / Watch IA

Purpose:

- create prestige
- hold stronger IP-like content
- enable premium positioning
- prepare future licensing and distribution

## Why The Products Must Be Separated Publicly

Users enter with specific intent.

A person looking for AI music behaves differently from a person looking for AI shorts or AI long-form storytelling.

If all experiences are mixed too early, the platform becomes conceptually interesting but behaviorally weak.

## Why The Core Must Be Unified Internally

A unified backend reduces:

- duplicated cost
- duplicated moderation complexity
- duplicated analytics work
- duplicated creator identity graphs
- duplicated fraud surfaces

It also enables:

- cross-front trust score
- future talent scoring
- shared storage policy
- consistent governance

## Financial Doctrine

Northstar must be self-sustaining before it becomes profit-seeking.

That means:

- phase-based cost ceilings
- controlled expansion
- storage lifecycle policy
- cost-aware media handling
- no unnecessary multi-front public burn

## Quality Doctrine

Northstar must not become a landfill of AI output.

Quality is enforced by:

- exposure limits
- trust score
- content similarity detection
- behavioral fraud detection
- per-front ranking logic
- archival or removal of dead content

## Content Doctrine

Northstar is for AI-native content.

Detection does not need to be perfect from day one, but the system must discourage misuse through:

- mandatory self-declaration
- metadata requirements
- reporting tools
- trust-based enforcement
- policy-backed penalties

## Readiness Gates

### Echo opens when

- core identity exists
- upload pipeline exists
- basic trust score exists
- catalog rules exist
- cost limits are defined
- command center can monitor launch health

### Pulse opens when

- Echo validates repeat usage
- creator return is healthy
- catalog quality is improving
- anti-spam is battle-tested
- command center can compare multi-front signals

### Lumen opens when

- creators with stronger followings already exist
- operational maturity is higher
- video cost controls are in place
- brand can support premium positioning
- command center supports premium-quality monitoring

## Time Governance

### Control Time Standard

- System control timezone: `UTC`
- Founder management mirror timezone: `America/Sao_Paulo`

All operational deadlines are stored in UTC and displayed to the founder in local time.

### Exact Review Clock

For every public launch, the system must schedule mandatory reviews at:

- T+6h
- T+24h
- T+72h
- T+7d
- T+14d
- T+30d
- T+45d
- T+60d
- T+90d

For every major release, the system must schedule:

- pre-release readiness review at T-72h
- go/no-go review at T-24h
- post-release health review at T+6h
- stabilization review at T+72h

### Exact Recurring Management Rhythm

- Daily pulse report: every day at 08:30 America/Sao_Paulo
- Daily infra and fraud digest: every day at 09:00 UTC
- Weekly operating review: every Monday at 09:00 America/Sao_Paulo
- Weekly product quality review: every Thursday at 15:00 America/Sao_Paulo
- Monthly executive review: first business day of each month at 10:00 America/Sao_Paulo
- Structural review: every 84 days at 10:00 America/Sao_Paulo
- Novelty planning review: every 42 days at 10:00 America/Sao_Paulo

## Communication Doctrine

The system must not only collect data.

It must communicate decisions in founder-ready language.

Every critical report should answer:

- what changed
- when it changed
- how severe it is
- what probably caused it
- what should be changed now
- what can wait
- what should not be touched yet

## Final Rule

Northstar is not building three apps for the sake of breadth.

Northstar is building one company that can support three category-defining products without collapsing under cost, spam or confusion.

Northstar will also operate with a permanent command layer so the founder can manage the ecosystem with clarity, timing precision and AI-assisted guidance.
